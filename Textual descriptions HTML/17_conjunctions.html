Conjunctions

Clauses are groups of words that contain a subject and a verb. Conjunctions are used to join clauses together to create more complex sentences and convey relationships between ideas.

Types of conjunctions:

There are two main types of conjunctions used to connect clauses:

  1. Coordinating conjunctions: These conjunctions connect independent clauses (main clauses) with equal importance. Common coordinating conjunctions are: for, and, nor, but, or, yet, and so. You can remember them using the acronym FANBOYS.
  2. Subordinating conjunctions: These conjunctions connect a dependent clause (which cannot stand alone) to an independent clause (which can stand alone). Examples of subordinating conjunctions include: because, since, although, if, when, while, and before.

Conjunctions play a crucial role in creating clear, coherent, and well-structured sentences by connecting clauses in the English language.
